Blessing for Challah

Tradition invites us to have a challah on our table, covered with a cloth. Some have two challot on the table to symbolize the double portion of manna the Israelites received in the Sinai Desert every Friday (Exodus 16:11–30). Remove the cover and recite:

בָּרוּךְ אַתָּה יְיָ, אֱלֹהֵֽינוּ מֶֽלֶךְ הָעוֹלָם, הַמּוֹצִיא לֶֽחֶם מִן הָאָֽרֶץ.

Baruch atah Adonai, Eloheinu Melech haolam, hamotzi lechem min haaretz.

Our praise to You, Eternal our God, Sovereign of the universe, who brings forth bread from the earth.
